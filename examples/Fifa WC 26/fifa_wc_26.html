Spain have scored only 8 goals but post the best defense (0.56 xGA) and a top-3 attack by xG — signs of under-conversion and possible positive finishing regression.

Squad behavior separates the contenders

France pair the best attack with the cleanest discipline (0.2 cards/match), while England carry real card risk (2.0/match) and Norway lean on one striker (Haaland = 64% of goals).

EvidenceGoal concentration and discipline are squad-composition risks that raise variance in single-elimination games.

Method & trust

Methodology

Context and evidence for Methodology.

Step 1

Data: 96 completed matches (Group + R32 + R16) from fifa_wc26, as of 2026-07-14.

Step 2

Features from player behavior (goals, assists, discipline) and squad composition (top-15 market value, age, caps, depth), plus team-match xG.

Step 3

Ratings: opponent-adjusted attack/defense on blended goals (0.6xG + 0.4 actual), shrunk toward a squad-strength prior (Elo + value + caps).

Step 4

Independent Poisson goals model; knockouts resolved with extra time and shootouts; neutral venue.

Step 5

10k Monte Carlo simulations through the reconstructed bracket (QF98=Spain-Belgium, QF100=Argentina-Switzerland).

Step 6

Validation: fit on group stage, predict 24 completed knockouts -> 66.7% accuracy, log-loss 0.54 vs 0.69 baseline.
